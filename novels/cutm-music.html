The Music of Catch Up To Myself

Here are many of the songs I listened to while writing Catch Up To Myself. For me, they capture the various emotions that Lem experiences during his coming of age journey.

The Catherine Wheel

Adam & Eve

I had this album in mind for Lem's drive out to the summer beach house.

"Intro"
"Future Boy"
"Delicious"
"Broken Nose" An amazing song about dropping all your emotional baggage and bad habits and moving forward in life, which is exactly the tipping point Lem is on the brink of on during his drive to the beach house.
"Ma Solituda"
"Goodbye" This one's a heartbreaker.
"For Dreaming"
"Outro" Another heartbreaker.

Chrome

I had this album in mind as what Lem would have heard when he put his earbuds in and went running on the beach, listening to the new album his friends just recorded.

  • "Kill Rhythm"  —  In fact, this is the opening track I had in mind for that moment. "The opening riffs of the album exploded into an expanse of sound as I hit the beach running, bringing back a small piece of my mushroom high. The amount of detail in each song was astounding."
  • "I Confess"
  • "Crank"
  • "Broken Head"
  • "Pain" —  This is sort of crazy, but I originally thought the lyrics were "Before the summer faire, I already knew." That one line bloomed in my mind and actually inspired the entire summer faire sequence in Catch Up To Myself. Only years later did I learn that the actual lyrics are, "Before the summer fell . . ."
  • "Strange Fruit" —  Has that feeling of summer freedom!"
  • "Chrome"
  • "The Nude" —  Lem's feelings for Monica."
  • "Fripp"
  • "Half Life"

Ferment

  • "I Want To Touch You"
  • "Black Metallic"
  • "Flower To Hide"

Happy Days

  • "Heal" — I actually didn't like this track much at first, but once I'd experienced the highest highs and lowest lows of my life, I realized how true to life it is.
  • "Eat My Dust You Insensitive Fuck" — Contrary to the aggressive title, this is actually the most peaceful track on the album. I always expected it to explode into noise, and it never does. Love it.
  • "Shocking"
  • "Love Tips Up"
  • "Judy Staring At The Sun" — This upbeat–sounding song is actually about the depravity of heroin addiction. But let's not think about that, shall we? It's more like a summertime song to me.
  • "Fizzy Love"
  • "Kill My Soul"

Like Cats and Dogs

  • "Heal 2" — Just when I'd learned to love the original "Heal," I discovered this alternate version, which sounds the same until it takes a different turn at the end. I found this jarring at first, but then it, too, grew on me, and now I'd be hard–pressed to say which version I prefer.
  • "Wish You Were Here" — Amazing Pink Floyd cover.
  • "Backwards Guitar"
  • "Tongue Twisted"
  • "High Heels" — I love the line, "I'm a bad decision maker." This is how Lem felt for most of the book.

Wishville

  • "Sparks Are Gonna Fly" — That amazing, energetic feeling of new love!
  • "Lifeline"
  • "Crème Caramel"
